Thandai Kulfi

Method

  1. Step 1 How to make Thandai masala This delicious kulfi is chiefly made using Thandai masala. However, if you do not have thandai masala at home, you can still prepare it easily using ingredients which can be easily be found in your kitchen. For making thandai masala, grind together equal quantities of almond, cashew nut, rose petal, black peppercorn, green cardamom, saffron, poppy seed and aniseed into a fine powder.

  2. Step 2 Boil the milk and reduce it to 2/3 of its quantity Put a deep-bottomed pan over medium flame and boil milk in it. Let it reduce to about 2/3 of its quantity is left. Then, add elaichi powder, sugar, thandai masala (step1) and condensed milk to it. Stir well and mix all the ingredients, and simmer for a while. Next, add kewra and rose water in the simmering milk and mix well. Be careful of the consistency of the milk.

  3. Step 3 Pour the mixture into desired moulds Once the milk thickens and you get a rich aroma, turn off the flame and transfer the mixture in a bowl. Let it cool for a while and add the mixture into the moulds of your choice. Put the moulds into the freezer and allow them to freeze for the proper shape of a kulfi.

  4. Step 4 Garnish with mint leaves and serve chilled Garnish with mint leaves and chopped pistachios, and serve chilled. (Tip: You can use fresh orange segment and milk quennel for a better taste and flavour.)

Ingredients

  • 1 litre milk 100 gm sugar
  • saffron as required 10 gm pistachios
  • 1 teaspoon rose water 40 gm condensed milk
  • 50 gm thandai powder 10 gm green cardamom
  • 2 drops kewra
